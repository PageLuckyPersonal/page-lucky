Song Covers

Song covers are a common way for musicians of all types to start a career. Taking a popular song and changing the arrangement or instruments used can greatly alter the sound of any common tune. Page Lucky performs a variety of different song covers from musical theater, jazz, opera, and old American songbook favorites. Here you'll find a list of sample song covers that are commonly requested along with a brief description about the history behind it.

Good Cover Songs

Page is always open to suggestions for popular song covers and requests. If you have a good song cover or would like to collaborate on a project, feel free to reach out to us on the contact page. A good song cover for any singer should fit their natural singing voice and make use of their best singing qualities. Page's voice is trained, melodic, and fluid, making it well suited to classical, old school, and popular jazz repertoire. She's also interested in new songs and compositions too.

I Could Have Danced All Night

Originally published in 1956, the I Could Have Danced All Night song was featured in the musical My Fair Lady. The music is by Frederick Loewe and the lyrics were written by Alan Jay Lerner. The character Eliza Doolittle sings this piece after dancing late into the evening with her friends and language teachers. Elated by her accomplishment, they dance the night away and she sings this song before going to bed.

A Spoonful Of Sugar

The Spoonful of Sugar song is from the 1964 Disney film Mary Poppins.It was composed by Robert B Sherman and Richard M Sherman. This magically inclined babysitter of sorts, wants to inspire the children to clean, and she does so with this fast and happy song.

Your Mother And Mine

This Your Mother And Mine song comes from the popular 1953 Disney movie Peter Pan. The character's name is Wendy Darling and she sings this song to the lost boys living inside the forest. By the end of the piece the lost boys are crying and yearning to return home to their mothers and family. Though not frequently covered by many singers, it remains one of Page's favorite songs to perform as it's both lyrical and charming.

I'll Never Stop Loving You

This song was made popular by Doris Day when she performed it in the 1955 film Love Me or Leave Me. The music is by Nicholas Bordzsky and the lyrics are by Sammy Cahn. There are many song covers of I'll Never Stop Loving You. Andy Williams, Bing Crosby, David Whitfield, Page Lucky, Dinah Washington, Nancy Wilson and Slim Whitman are some examples of artists who have covered them.

But Not For Me

The song But Not For Me was written by George Gershwin and Ira Gershwin. Featured in the musical Girl Crazy in 1930, it was sung by Ginger Rogers. The song became more popular after Ella Fitzgerald's 1959 But Not For Me song cover was released. Many people know the song now still through Ella Fitzgerald's CD and also from the Judy Garland and Doris Day song covers too.

Edelweiss

Originally published in 1959, this song was used in the musical The Sound of Music, written by Rodgers and Hammerstein. The edelweirss is a small whtie flower that's grows in the area that the movie is set in, in the high Alps. The character that sings it is the Captain Georg von Trapp, played by actor Theodore Bikel. The Edelweiss song has since been covered in many different styles and by many different artists.

Have Yourself A Merry Little Christmas

Written in 1943 this popular christmas song was written by Hugh Martin and Ralph Blane. It's now a standard top 10 Christmas song for many people around the world. It was sung and performed by Judy garland in the musical Meet Me in St. Louis, produced by MGM. The song is such a popular one to cover that new versions of it are released every year.

I'm Dreaming of a White Christmas

In 1942 Bing Crosby sang I'm Dreaming of a White Christmas on a single record that's now sold over 50 million copies. It was written by Irving berlin, but the exact day it was written isn't none for sure. There are too many I'm Dreaming of a White christmas song covers to list here, and new versions are published every year. It's a great song not only for the recording studio artist, by for live performances too.

I'm Getting Myself Ready For You

This Cole Porter song comes from the movie High Society, based on The Philadelphia Story by Philip Barry. The movie released in 1956 featuring Cole Porter's song I'm Getting Myself Ready For You. A witty and comical romance duet, it's not often covered or performed by many artists now. True Old Hollywood and Old American Songbook fans are familiar with it and like it.

True Love

Written by Cole Porter, this old American classic was sung by Bing Crosby and Grace Kelly in the film High Society. The single later sung by only Bing Crosby with full orchestra was a hit single that's still popular now. There are many True Love song cover arrangements and though not as popular as some of Cole Porter's other songs, it still maintains a position in the spotlight today.
