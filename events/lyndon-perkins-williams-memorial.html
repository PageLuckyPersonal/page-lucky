Lyndon Perkins Williams Memorial

Trained classical singer Page Lucky joins Allegro Singers again for a group performance at The Williams Memorial Center along with soprano Monique Mcdonald and tenor Joseph Di Salle. This was a free opera performance for Memorial Center staff and residents funded by Williams Memorial management as a treat for their guests. This was Pages second performance at the Williams Center with a different group of singers.

While Pages first performance at Williams was Russian repertoire heavy with a sprinkling of broadways biggest hits, the second time featured beloved duets from Verdi's operas Rigoletto and La Traviata with tenor Joseph Salle at her side. Verdi's love duets were followed by Page singing "I Feel Pretty", which was performed at her first Williams performance and was brought back by popular demand by attendees. Inna Leytush treated the crowd to famous piano pieces by Tchaikovsky, Wagner, and Brahms. Monique Mcdonald also performed some newly discovered works that are religious based and air on the more modern side of music composition. The opera singer trio wrapped the evening up with a group singalong of Que Sera Sera.

Amongst this weeks song requests were Some Enchanted Evening, selections from Phantom of the Opera, and also popular songs from The Sound of Music. While the Williams group audiences always enjoy famous opera arias, they were eager on both occasions to add more theater and jazz tunes to their new repertoire request list.
