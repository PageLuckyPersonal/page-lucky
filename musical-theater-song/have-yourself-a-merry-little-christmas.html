Have Yourself A Merry Little Christmas Cover

Watch Page Lucky's new version of Have Yourself A Merry Little Christmas! Page's focus for this project keeps the traditional romantic styling listeners love, while introducing modern jazz elements. Listen to the beginning of her cover Have Yourself A Merry Little Christmas and enjoy the original music box beginning, which changes to a thicker jazz sound a few bars in. The goal was to stay true to the famous Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas Judy Garland version, while adding modern jazz additions that wouldn't be distracting. When listening to the Christmas song, you should feel like you're experiencing an old favorite, with a new voice. The singer really wanted to blend a classical vocal style with fully sung jazz technique. When you think of jazz versions, the Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas Michael Buble version comes to mind. Frank Sinatra similarly has a jazz versions of the song.

New Have Yourself A Merry Little Christmas

What makes Page's Christmas song cover different is the true blending of both classical and jazz styles. Frank Sinatra and Michael Buble's Christmas songs use a sung and mixed sprechstimme jazz style. This new Christmas song is different because it's sung with all of the traditional classical elements, but with fully sung jazz styling. That means jazz style is ever present. You'll hear rubato phrasing and lower register singing(without the full chest voice as is tradition). Straight tone singing is also used to create a different color midway through. The result is a milky finish that stays in the same vocal area for a seamless sound. It's manages to maintain everything you love about the original version, while seamlessly introducing contemporary moments. You can hear modern phrasing, chromatic passages, and Puccini style rubato. As a classical trained singer who specializes in jazz, it features all of Page's strengths.

Starting from the single music box line, it then grows to the climax with chromatic phrasing and thicker jazz chords. The new Christmas song then returns to a simple single note, ending with a romantic period piano vocalise. It's different, new, and exciting. Enjoy this easy listening Christmas song favorite that allows you to reflect while feeling the excitement we all feel at the holidays.
